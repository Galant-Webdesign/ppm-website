# PPM – Pro Performance Maintenance
## Services Content

---

## 1. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the service content direction for the PPM – Pro Performance Maintenance website.

It should be used as the source of truth for:
- service descriptions,
- service card copy,
- service page content,
- service-specific imagery,
- SEO keywords,
- CTA direction,
- audience targeting,
- website prompts,
- future landing page expansion.

The services should be presented as premium property finishing and maintenance solutions, not as generic handyman tasks.

---

## 2. Core Service Categories

The website should present five core services:

```text
Painting
Plastering
Carpentry
Strata
Remedial
```

Important spelling note:

The correct professional spelling should be:

```text
Remedial
```

Avoid using:

```text
Remidial
```

unless the client specifically requires that spelling.

---

## 3. Service Positioning

PPM’s services should be framed around:

- premium finishes,
- careful preparation,
- clean execution,
- property value improvement,
- reliability,
- long-term maintenance,
- residential and commercial capability,
- organised communication,
- attention to detail,
- trust in high-value properties.

Do not position the services as cheap, quick-fix handyman work.

Preferred positioning:

```text
Premium painting, plastering, carpentry, strata and remedial maintenance services for Sydney residential, commercial and managed properties.
```

---

## 4. Service Audience Overview

PPM serves two main audience groups.

### Residential Clients

Includes:
- homeowners,
- property investors,
- luxury property owners,
- renovators,
- sellers preparing property for market,
- landlords needing reliable maintenance,
- families improving or repairing their homes.

Residential clients care about:
- trust,
- clean work,
- careful protection of the home,
- premium finish,
- reliable timing,
- clear quotes,
- minimal disruption,
- good communication.

---

### Commercial and Managed Property Clients

Includes:
- builders,
- real estate agencies,
- property managers,
- strata managers,
- commercial property owners,
- facility managers,
- developers,
- businesses.

Commercial clients care about:
- reliability,
- professionalism,
- insurance/licensing,
- repeatable quality,
- minimal disruption,
- clear process,
- commercial presentation,
- timely completion,
- ability to handle jobs of different sizes.

---

## 5. Global Service Tone

All service descriptions should sound:

- professional,
- premium,
- confident,
- direct,
- practical,
- trustworthy,
- clear.

Avoid:
- exaggerated sales language,
- “cheap” or “budget” positioning,
- overpromising,
- handyman-style wording,
- generic contractor clichés,
- vague copy like “we do it all”.

Preferred language:
- premium finish,
- surface preparation,
- clean execution,
- reliable maintenance,
- long-lasting result,
- professional team,
- careful process,
- Sydney properties,
- residential and commercial,
- strata and property management support.

# 51. Service Cards — Recommended Homepage Format

Use this structure for homepage service cards:

```text
[Service Image]
[Service Name]
[Short Description]
[CTA or Hover State]
```

Example:

```text
Painting

Interior and exterior painting delivered with careful preparation, clean execution and a premium finish.

Request Painting Quote
```

Cards should feel premium and architectural, not like generic icon cards.

---

# 52. Service Section Layout Options

Recommended layout options:

## Option A — Five-Card Grid

Use a clean grid:
- 3 cards on first row,
- 2 larger cards on second row,
- strong images,
- hover reveal copy.

Best for:
- clear overview,
- homepage.

---

## Option B — Editorial Split Layout

Use:
- large service image on one side,
- service list on the other,
- active service changes image/content.

Best for:
- premium feel,
- less clutter,
- stronger hierarchy.

---

## Option C — Horizontal Scroll / Sticky Service Showcase

Use:
- sticky left heading,
- scrolling service panels,
- each service has image and copy.

Best for:
- more dynamic website,
- advanced animation.

# 55. Service Proof Requirements

Where possible, connect services to proof:

- before/after painting project,
- plaster repair example,
- carpentry/deck repair testimonial,
- strata maintenance photo,
- remedial patching image,
- testimonials mentioning cleanliness, punctuality and professionalism.

This helps avoid the services feeling like unsupported claims.

---

# 56. Service Copy Rules

Use short service copy on the homepage.

Use longer service copy only on individual service pages.

Homepage copy should be:
- short,
- scannable,
- benefit-focused,
- clear.

Individual service pages can include:
- what the service includes,
- who it is for,
- common problems,
- process,
- before/after,
- FAQ,
- CTA.
