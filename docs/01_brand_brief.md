# PPM – Pro Performance Maintenance  
## Brand Brief for Premium Website Project

---

## 1. Brand Name

**PPM – Pro Performance Maintenance**

Supporting descriptor currently used by the brand:

**Group of Trades**

Recommended website positioning:

**Premium painting, finishing and maintenance partner for Sydney properties.**

---

## 2. Location

**Sydney, NSW, Australia**

The website should clearly communicate that PPM operates in Sydney and serves residential, commercial and strata property clients across New South Wales.

---

## 3. Core Business Definition

PPM is a premium property finishing, painting, plastering, carpentry, strata and remedial maintenance company.

The company provides high-quality trade services for homeowners, property investors, builders, real estate agencies, property managers, strata managers and commercial clients.

PPM should be positioned as a professional property improvement and finishing partner, not as a generic contractor or handyman service.

---

## 4. Brand Positioning

### PPM is not:

- A handyman business
- A budget painting contractor
- A generic maintenance company
- A cheap trade service provider
- A casual renovation helper
- A cluttered, low-end contractor brand

### PPM is:

A premium finishing, painting and maintenance partner trusted by Sydney property owners, businesses, builders, real estate professionals and strata managers.

PPM delivers refined, reliable and detail-focused work across painting, plastering, carpentry, strata maintenance and remedial services.

The brand should feel serious, organised, high-quality and trustworthy.

---

## 5. Brand Promise

PPM helps property owners and businesses improve, protect and elevate their spaces through premium finishing, painting and maintenance services delivered with precision, care and professionalism.

### Suggested core promise:

**Residential and commercial projects, done right.**

### Expanded promise:

**Premium painting, finishing and maintenance services for Sydney homes, strata properties and commercial spaces.**

---

## 6. Target Audience

### Residential Audience

- Homeowners
- Property investors
- Luxury property owners
- Home renovators
- Owners preparing a property for sale
- Clients who need reliable painting, plastering, repairs or finishing work

### Commercial Audience

- Builders
- Real estate agencies
- Property managers
- Strata managers
- Commercial clients
- Developers
- Facility managers
- Businesses requiring reliable maintenance support

---

## 7. Audience Needs

### Residential clients need:

- A trustworthy team inside their home
- Clean, tidy and careful work
- High-quality finishes
- Reliable communication
- Accurate quoting
- On-time execution
- A premium final result
- Confidence that the work will improve the property’s value and appearance

### Commercial and strata clients need:

- Dependable service partner
- Professional presentation
- Ability to handle jobs of different sizes
- Consistent workmanship
- Insurance, qualifications and accountability
- Minimal disruption
- Efficient project delivery
- Long-term maintenance reliability
- Clear communication with owners, managers or tenants

---

## 8. Core Services

The website should present five main service categories.

---

### 1. Painting

Residential and commercial painting services, including interior and exterior painting, repainting, touch-ups, cabinetry, wood finishes and wall finishes.

**Positioning:**  
Premium preparation, clean execution and long-lasting finishes for residential and commercial properties.

**Tone:**  
Refined, precise, clean, finish-focused.

---

### 2. Plastering

Plastering, plasterboard installation, skimming, wall repairs, patching, renovation support and damage repair.

**Positioning:**  
Smooth surfaces, precise repair work and flawless preparation for painting or finishing.

**Tone:**  
Technical, clean, detail-oriented, reliable.

---

### 3. Carpentry

Carpentry services for functional and aesthetic property improvements, including timber repairs, fixtures, trims, cabinetry-related support, doors, decking and general property upgrades.

**Positioning:**  
Accurate, durable and refined carpentry that improves both function and appearance.

**Tone:**  
Practical, precise, premium, durable.

---

### 4. Strata

Maintenance and improvement services for shared residential and commercial buildings, including common areas, hallways, exterior and interior upkeep, repairs and ongoing support.

**Positioning:**  
Reliable and organised strata maintenance for property managers, strata committees and building owners.

**Tone:**  
Professional, dependable, systematic, low-disruption.

---

### 5. Remedial

Remedial repair work for damaged surfaces, cracks, storm or water damage, building wear, concrete/masonry issues and corrective maintenance.

**Positioning:**  
Professional repair, surface restoration and long-term property protection.

**Tone:**  
Technical, corrective, trustworthy, solution-focused.

**Important note:**  
Use the spelling **“Remedial”** on the final website unless the client specifically requires the spelling “Remidial”.

---

## 9. Key Trust Factors

The website should communicate that PPM is:

- Fully insured and licensed
- Experienced and qualified
- Professional
- Careful and systematic
- Clean and tidy
- Reliable
- Detail-oriented
- Able to provide free quotes
- Capable of jobs of different sizes
- Experienced across residential and commercial work
- Trusted by previous clients
- Recommended for quality, punctuality and professionalism

These trust factors should be integrated into the website through refined credibility sections, concise copy, testimonials, project examples and premium visual hierarchy.

Avoid presenting these points as cheap generic icons only. They should feel like part of a premium brand system.

- Organised

## 12. Visual Style Direction

The website should feel like:

**“Apple meets premium architectural contractor.”**

The design should combine:

- Premium minimalism
- Architectural design language
- Editorial layouts
- Modern luxury
- Contemporary Australian architecture inspiration
- Large whitespace
- Strong typography
- High-quality photography
- Clean section rhythm
- Subtle motion
- Precise spacing
- Premium service presentation

---

## 13. Visual Style to Avoid

Avoid:

- Generic SaaS aesthetics
- Cheap stock-photo layouts
- Construction tape clichés
- Yellow-black contractor branding
- Excessive gradients
- Overly corporate templates
- Cluttered interfaces
- Cartoonish trade graphics
- Loud icons
- Messy textures
- Low-quality before/after layouts
- Random unbranded workers
- Visual clichés associated with cheap renovation services

---

## 14. Colour Palette

Primary colour palette:

- **#000d20** — Main colour
- **#0f2c4d** — Secondary navy
- **#cbcbcb** — Neutral grey
- **#e9e9e9** — Light grey
- **#ffffff** — White

### Colour Usage Guidance

#### #000d20
Use as the main premium brand colour for hero backgrounds, dark sections, CTA backgrounds, branded transitions and paint effects.

#### #0f2c4d
Use as a secondary navy for supporting dark UI, hover states, subtle sections and depth.

#### #cbcbcb
Use for borders, dividers, subdued text, icon lines and architectural UI elements.

#### #e9e9e9
Use for light section backgrounds, surface texture references, cards and neutral content blocks.

#### #ffffff
Use for typography on dark backgrounds, premium whitespace, CTA contrast and clean layouts.

The palette should feel restrained, architectural and premium. Avoid unnecessary gradients. If any gradient is used, it should be extremely subtle and functional, not decorative.

---

## 15. Typography Direction

Typography should feel modern, clean and architectural.

Recommended direction:

- Strong sans-serif headings
- Clean grotesk or geometric sans-serif body text
- Large editorial headlines
- Clear hierarchy
- Tight but readable spacing
- Minimal decorative typography

### Heading style:

- Confident
- Short
- Premium
- Direct
- Spacious

### Body copy style:

- Clear
- Professional
- Simple
- Trustworthy
- Service-focused

---

## 16. Photography Direction

Photography should be realistic, high-quality and service-based.

Use images of:

- PPM workers in navy branded workwear
- Workers actively painting, plastering, repairing, maintaining or doing carpentry
- Clean residential interiors
- Contemporary Australian homes
- Strata/common areas
- Commercial property environments
- Close-ups of paint, plaster, wall surfaces, tools and finishes
- Before/after transformations

Workers should wear:

- Navy workwear visually aligned with the brand
- PPM logo in white
- Clean but realistic trade clothing
- Gloves and relevant tools when appropriate

Photography should avoid:

- Fake-looking AI hands/tools
- Overly polished unrealistic stock images
- Messy construction chaos
- Cheap handyman visuals
- Yellow/black contractor cliché colours
- Workers without visible brand connection

---

The website should make PPM look like a premium, serious, reliable and modern property finishing partner.

The visitor should immediately feel:

- This company is professional.
- This company is organised.
- This company can be trusted in high-value properties.
- This company works with both homeowners and commercial clients.

- This company delivers clean, high-quality results.

**PPM – Pro Performance Maintenance is a premium Sydney-based painting, finishing and maintenance partner for residential, commercial and strata properties. The brand combines practical trade expertise with refined presentation, clean execution, reliable communication and detail-focused workmanship.
